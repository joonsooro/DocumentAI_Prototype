§ 00

Method

Criteria walked top-to-bottom from app/spec.html: Behaviour Contract §5 (Always, Ask First, Never), FAQ §3 (HAPPY · EDGE · RED), Substrate §9, and §6 Constraints. Each criterion carries a single binding source and one of four kinds.

Self-check rows are mechanical — a count, a regex, a type check, a literal-string presence test. The Judge applies them by reading source or running the test. Judge-required rows need an independent reviewer's read of the artifact and a one-sentence Gulf tag on every FAIL. Eval-level kill rows are numeric, time-bounded, and structurally separate — failing any one means the build fails its release bar regardless of other passes. OQ-E rows are criteria the spec gestures at but does not operationalise; the verdict in Step 2 will be N/A with a pointer to the open question.

N/A is not a pass and not a fail. It means the spec has not authorised a verdict yet.

§ 05

How the Judge applies these

One verdict per (criterion × applicable pick) in app/eval-results.html. Picks are the artifacts the running app produces: the three routes, the agent modules, the view-models, the eval suite output, and the typecheck / lint state.

Every FAIL row names a Gulf — Comprehension (model misread the spec), Specification (spec was ambiguous), or Generalization (right for this case, wrong reasoning for the next) — and a one-sentence why. Every N/A row names the OQ-E# id and the suggested resolution date.
